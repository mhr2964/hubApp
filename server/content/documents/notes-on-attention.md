# Notes on Attention

What you pay attention to is what you become.

This is not a metaphor. Attention is the mechanism by which experience becomes identity. The things you return to, voluntarily or not, are shaping the person you'll be in ten years.

Most of us don't choose what we attend to. We let the loudest thing win. The loudest thing is rarely the most important.

Reclaiming attention isn't about discipline. It's about caring enough about something to protect time for it. The people who make things — real things, lasting things — are not more talented. They are more protective of where their attention goes.
