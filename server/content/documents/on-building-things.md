# On Building Things

The act of making something from nothing is the closest we get to understanding what we are.

Most things that exist in the world were made by someone who didn't know exactly what they were doing. They started anyway. The blueprint came later — if it came at all.

There's a version of building that waits for permission: for the right tools, the right time, the right amount of knowledge. That version doesn't build much. The other version starts with what's available and figures out the rest in motion.

The hard part isn't the making. It's trusting that the thing you're making is worth the mess it requires.
