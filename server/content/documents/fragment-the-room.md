# Fragment: The Room

He kept the window open even in winter. Said it reminded him that the world outside was still moving.

The room was small. A bed, a desk, a chair he never used because the bed was closer to the window. Stacks of paper that had not been moved in months but were not forgotten — only deferred. A radiator that knocked twice at midnight and then went quiet for the rest of the night.

He liked it best in the hour before sunrise, when the sky had not yet decided what color to be, and the air coming through the window carried a temperature with no name.
