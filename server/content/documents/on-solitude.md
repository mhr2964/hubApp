# On Solitude

There is a difference between loneliness and being alone with yourself.

Loneliness is a kind of distance — the gap between who you want to be near and where you actually are. Solitude is closer to its opposite: a deliberate proximity, a willingness to keep your own company without flinching.

The strange part is that you can be lonely in a room full of people and at peace in a room with no one. The room is incidental. The relationship is to yourself.
